Album Experience

House of Balloons

House of Balloons feels like a beautiful nightmare you do not want to wake up from — a suspended world of late-night emptiness, pleasure, guilt, and quiet self-destruction.

beauty and emptiness drift together after the party is already over
Floating forms drift like the remains of a party that ended hours ago.

The Core Feeling

Being surrounded by people but still feeling alone. Beauty and indulgence are everywhere, but underneath it all there is detachment, guilt, and emotional emptiness.

Late-night emptiness
Pleasure & guilt
Isolation
Seductive unease
Beautiful nightmare

Sound & Themes

The production is dark, echo-heavy, and haunting. The vocals feel distant, almost ghost-like, while the songs move through desire, excess, toxic attachment, and the emptiness hidden beneath escape.

Toxic love & detachment Relationships feel intimate but emotionally unstable, seductive but hollow.
Drugs, partying, escapism Pleasure becomes a way to avoid pain, but the high never truly fixes the emptiness.
Power and vulnerability The album constantly shifts between control, temptation, fragility, and emotional distance.

Track Atmosphere

These tracks feel less like a playlist and more like rooms inside the same haunted night. Hover over them as if moving through moments of the album.

2019/20 Season

Historical Context

The 2019/20 season was one of the most turbulent seasons in the modern history of FC Barcelona. Barcelona began the campaign under coach Ernesto Valverde and entered the season as defending La Liga champions, with star players like Lionel Messi, Luis Suárez, and Antoine Griezmann leading the squad. Despite inconsistent performances early in the season, Barcelona still managed to stay near the top of the league, but the team struggled with injuries, defensive problems, and criticism over their style of play. After a shocking defeat to Atlético Madrid in the 2020 Supercopa de España semi-final, Valverde was dismissed in January 2020 and replaced by Quique Setién. Under Setién, Barcelona initially showed flashes of improvement but soon lost momentum, eventually finishing second in La Liga behind Real Madrid. The season ended disastrously in Europe when Barcelona suffered an infamous 8–2 defeat to FC Bayern Munich in the UEFA Champions League quarter-final during the COVID-19-affected tournament in Lisbon, one of the heaviest defeats in the club’s history. The humiliating loss triggered a major crisis at the club, leading to Setién’s dismissal, internal turmoil, and even Messi publicly requesting to leave the club that summer.

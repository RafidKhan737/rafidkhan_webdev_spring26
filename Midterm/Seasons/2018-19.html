2018/19 Season

Historical Context

The 2018/19 season was another strong domestic campaign for FC Barcelona under head coach Ernesto Valverde. Led by the brilliance of Lionel Messi, who finished the season as the top scorer in La Liga and the UEFA Champions League, Barcelona dominated Spanish football by winning La Liga with 87 points, finishing far ahead of their rivals. The team featured key players such as Luis Suárez, Gerard Piqué, Sergio Busquets, Jordi Alba, and Ivan Rakitić, while new signings like Arthur Melo helped strengthen the midfield. Messi also played a crucial role in Barcelona’s run to the UEFA Champions League semifinals, including a famous 3–0 first-leg victory against Liverpool at Camp Nou where he scored two goals, one being a stunning free kick. However, the season ended in disappointment in Europe when Barcelona suffered a dramatic 4–0 defeat at Anfield, losing the tie 4–3 on aggregate and being eliminated from the competition. The club also reached the Copa del Rey final but lost 2–1 to Valencia, meaning Barcelona finished the season with La Liga as their only major trophy despite dominating much of the year.

2021/22 Season

Historical Context

The 2021/22 season was one of the most transitional and difficult periods in FC Barcelona’s modern history. Following severe financial problems and the departure of club legend Lionel Messi in August 2021, Barcelona entered the season with uncertainty under manager Ronald Koeman. Poor performances early in the campaign, including heavy defeats in the UEFA Champions League group stage against Bayern Munich, led to Koeman’s dismissal in October. Club legend Xavi Hernández was then appointed as the new head coach and began rebuilding the team’s identity and playing style. Under Xavi, Barcelona improved significantly in the second half of the season, with new signings like Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang and the emergence of young talents such as Pedri and Gavi. Although the team finished 2nd in La Liga behind Real Madrid and were knocked out of the Champions League group stage for the first time in over two decades, the latter part of the season showed signs of recovery and optimism as Barcelona began rebuilding for the future.

2015/16 Season

Historical Context

The 2015/16 season was another highly successful campaign for FC Barcelona under manager Luis Enrique, following their historic treble the year before. Barcelona continued their dominance in Spain with the famous attacking trio of Lionel Messi, Luis Suárez, and Neymar— known as “MSN”—leading the attack and scoring a huge number of goals throughout the season. Barcelona won La Liga with 91 points, finishing ahead of Real Madrid and Atlético Madrid, and also captured the Copa del Rey after defeating Sevilla FC 2–0 in the final. Earlier in the season, Barcelona also won the UEFA Super Cup in a thrilling 5–4 victory against Sevilla FC and lifted the FIFA Club World Cup by beating CA River Plate 3–0 in the final, with Suárez scoring twice. Although Barcelona were eliminated in the quarterfinals of the UEFA Champions League by Atlético Madrid, the season was still considered a major success as the club secured a domestic double and five trophies overall, further cementing the dominance of the MSN era.
